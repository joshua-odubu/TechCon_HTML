Our History

TechCon began in 2005 as a small gathering of developers and engineers eager to explore the future of technology. Over the years, it has grown into an internationally recognized conference attracting innovators, entrepreneurs, and tech leaders from around the globe.

Our Mission

TechCon’s mission is to accelerate Africa’s digital transformation by creating a platform where stakeholders from various industries can learn, collaborate, and innovate using emerging technologies.

Past Speakers

Dalene Deale

Dalene Deale is a fintech strategist and regulatory expert who helps banks, fintechs, and regulators align compliance with commercial growth. As founder of Business Growth Hacking (Pty) Ltd, she has shaped national initiatives like SARB DFID and payment compliance programs in South Africa. Known for her clarity, creativity, and human-centered approach, Dalene transforms complex regulatory topics into strategic opportunities. Beyond her professional work, she’s pursuing a doctorate in tech adoption and co-authoring a poetry book with AI — blending logic with purpose.

Louis Yaw Afful

Louis Yaw Afful is an internationally recognized trade practitioner and expert on the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA). He is the Executive Director of the APN Group, the leading NGO in Africa focused on private sector advocacy, sensitization, and implementation of AfCFTA. A pioneer in AfCFTA advocacy across Ghana and Africa, Louis has played key roles in policy formulation, research, and capacity building. He serves on multiple expert technical working groups, including for the African Union and Ghana's Ministry of Trade and Industry, and has consulted for organizations like ACET and the Rockefeller Foundation. Formerly a finance manager and country director in international organizations, he is also a UNYA Ghana Ambassador on Sustainable Trade. Louis is a sought-after international speaker featured on platforms like BBC and CNBC and holds board positions with global and national bodies, including SEEDS Dubai, WCO, and the Ghana Chamber of SMEs.

Rhursh Ramhurry

Rhursh is the founder of W3OLABS, a Web 3.0 ecosystem builder that connects startups with investors and helps traditional businesses transition into the Web 3.0 space. Over the past two and a half years, he has worked with global clients like Cardano, ICP, and Polygon, contributing to strategy, software development, and product launches. Previously, Rhursh spent four years in corporate finance at Standard Bank and EY, managing major M&A deals. He later led growth at BlackBrick, scaling it from concept to a national presence.
